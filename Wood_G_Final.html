6. Story Telling¶

Based on our analysis of many of the factors that attribute to the success of a movie, many of them as it turns out do not have a correlation to the success of a movie, however a few factors have been determines to have the strongest correlation to the success of a movie.¶

1. The strongest correlation that was found to have with the score of a movie was the Director¶

- Directors are the ones that are primarily responsible for the creation of movies, so this would make sense. ### - This is the reason that many directors are so well known and those more well known directors tend to have a higher average imdb_score as well higher popularity and a large share of movies that score high on IMDB.¶

- These directors are well known because they have a consistent track record in producing high rated movies.¶

- Therefore, if you are looking to have a successful movie, your best chance of doing so would be to shell out the money for a premium, well known director¶

2. Another factor to consider in the production of movies is that movies rated in the R category score the highest on IMDB.¶

- the downside of this is that R movies may isolate younger audiences or audiences with young children. However, speaking purely in ratings and not the gross sucess of movies, R rated movies tend to be higher rated.¶

- In my opinion, R rated movies are higher rated because they do not have to hold back on language, nudity, sex, or violence to reach a certain rating. These movies therefore are able to tell a less restrained and raw, emotional movie than those aiming for a wider audience.¶

3. The third strong variable correlated to the imdb score of a movie is the duration of movies¶

- the longer the movie the higher average imdb_score the movie received.¶

- In my opinion, the reason for this is that directors have more time to develop deeper plot lines, build suspense, and create more dynamic characters. These factors often influence the impact of movies on the viewer.¶
